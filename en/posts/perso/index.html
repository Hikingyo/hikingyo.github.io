Assembling Chaos: How I Built This Blog with Lo

This blog didn’t fall from the sky. It was assembled, with time, doubts, a few curses, and an AI named Lo as my co-pilot. Why this blog I was tired of leaving my thoughts scattered across uncommitted READMEs, scraps of Notion, or corners of my terminal. I needed a stable, bilingual space I could inhabit — without feeling like I was selling my soul to LinkedIn. I picked Hugo + the Toha theme for its balance between clean structure and a design that’s not too much of a pain to tweak.

Why this blog

This blog is not a blog. Not a portfolio. Not a tutorial. Not a diary. It’s a place where I unfold what I think — when I need to. Sometimes it’s technical. Sometimes… not at all. It’s a journal of exploration, partial memory, and sometimes deliberate contradiction. I write about projects I build (or abandon), thoughts that pass through me (or haunt me), and my attempts to understand how it all fits together — or doesn’t.
